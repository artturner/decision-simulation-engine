# Branching Scenario Design

## Core Design Principles

### Meaningful Choice Architecture
Create decision points that require genuine analysis and application of knowledge:

**Choice Construction:**
- Present 3-5 plausible options per decision point (avoid 2 or 6+)
- Make all options defensible from some perspective to prevent gaming
- Reflect actual options available to practitioners in similar situations
- Use clear, professional language without telegraphing the "correct" answer
- Ensure choices require weighing trade-offs, not identifying obvious answers

**Critical Decision Points:**
Identify moments where different approaches lead to significantly different outcomes. Base these on:
- Real-world practitioner interviews and observations
- Actual case studies from the target domain
- Moments of genuine professional uncertainty or ethical complexity

### Realistic Consequences and Feedback

**Consequence Design:**
- Connect consequences logically to decisions (cause-and-effect must be clear)
- Show multiple levels: immediate results, delayed implications, and ripple effects
- Include both intended and unintended outcomes
- Reveal emotional/relational impacts alongside practical outcomes
- Avoid overly punitive consequences that discourage exploration

**Feedback Mechanisms:**
- Provide immediate, specific feedback explaining why consequences occurred
- Include explanatory context: "This happened because..."
- Offer guidance for future similar situations
- Frame negative outcomes as learning opportunities, not failures
- Show pathways for recovery and improvement

### Progressive Complexity and Scaffolding

**Complexity Progression:**
- Start with straightforward applications of fundamental concepts
- Gradually increase along multiple dimensions:
  - Number of factors to consider
  - Ambiguity of available information
  - Time pressure intensity
  - Stakes and consequences significance

**Scaffolding Strategy:**
- Early scenarios: Extensive guidance, access to references, consultation options
- Middle scenarios: Moderate support, structured reflection prompts
- Later scenarios: Independent decision-making with minimal support
- Fade scaffolding as learners demonstrate competence

### Authentic Context and Stakeholders

**Situational Authenticity:**
- Accurately represent physical, social, and organizational contexts
- Include realistic constraints: resource limitations, time pressures, competing priorities
- Reflect genuine decision-making criteria from the target domain

**Stakeholder Representation:**
- Include the full ecosystem of affected parties (obvious and hidden)
- Give each stakeholder realistic motivations and constraints
- Show legitimate but potentially conflicting interests
- Avoid stereotypes; create multidimensional characters
- Reflect diverse workplace realities and communication styles

### Reflection and Metacognition

**Reflection Integration:**
- Place reflection points throughout, not just at the end
- Mid-scenario pauses allow strategy reassessment
- Prompt examination of:
  - Information considered or ignored
  - Criteria used to evaluate options
  - Assumptions about stakeholders/consequences
  - Emotions or biases influencing choices

**Forward-Looking Reflection:**
- End with personal action plans for real-world application
- Encourage identification of specific behavioral changes
- Compare approaches with peers or experts when possible

## Structural Design Patterns

### Branching Architecture Types

**Simple Branching:**
- Linear progression with occasional detours that reconverge
- Use for: Procedural training, established best practices
- Advantage: Easier development and maintenance

**Complex Branching:**
- Multiple pathways leading to different outcomes
- Use for: Critical thinking, ethical decision-making, leadership
- Requires: Sophisticated planning, more development resources

**Adaptive Branching:**
- Adjusts difficulty based on learner performance
- Use for: Diverse learner populations, competency-based training
- Requires: Performance tracking, conditional logic

**Hybrid Approach (Recommended):**
- Simple branching for foundational concepts
- Complex branching for advanced applications
- Match structural complexity to pedagogical requirements

### Information Architecture

**Just-in-Time Delivery:**
- Present relevant details precisely when needed for decisions
- Prevent information overload while ensuring informed choices

**Layered Information:**
- Surface level: Essential details for basic decision-making
- Deeper layers: Additional context for comprehensive understanding
- Accommodates different learning styles and experience levels

**Reference Systems:**
- Provide ongoing access to policies, procedures, frameworks
- Keep easily accessible without disrupting flow
- Use consistent formatting and navigation patterns

## Content Development Guidelines

### Narrative and Character Development

**Character Construction:**
- Create multidimensional characters with realistic motivations
- Avoid stereotypical portrayals
- Position protagonist at appropriate authority level for target audience
- Give protagonist sufficient agency for meaningful decisions

**Supporting Characters:**
- Mentors: Provide guidance and expertise
- Colleagues: Offer different perspectives
- Clients/Customers: Present challenges and requirements
- Supervisors: Establish expectations and feedback

**Conflict Sources:**
- Competing priorities and resource constraints
- Ethical dilemmas and interpersonal disagreements
- Time pressures mirroring real-world challenges
- Avoid artificial dramatic devices

### Dialogue Writing

**Professional Authenticity:**
- Use terminology and language patterns from target profession
- Reflect communication styles typical of the context
- Maintain character voice consistency throughout

**Emotional Intelligence:**
- Express appropriate emotions for situations
- Provide opportunities to recognize and respond to emotional cues
- Include subtext requiring reading between the lines

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Over-Complexity
**Symptoms:**
- Too many choices (>5) at decision points
- Information overload obscuring key factors
- Dozens of possible pathways creating confusion

**Solutions:**
- Limit choices to 3-5 per decision point
- Use progressive disclosure for information
- Focus on most important decision points only
- Minimize extraneous cognitive load

### Artificial Scenarios
**Symptoms:**
- Oversimplified situations with obvious answers
- Stereotypical characters and predictable patterns
- Unrealistic consequences disconnected from decisions

**Solutions:**
- Conduct thorough research: interviews, observations, case studies
- Involve SMEs throughout design process
- Test with target audience for authenticity feedback
- Reflect genuine complexity and ambiguity

### Poor Feedback Design
**Symptoms:**
- Delayed or absent feedback
- Generic feedback ("good choice" without explanation)
- Overly punitive consequences discouraging exploration
- Inconsistent consequence logic

**Solutions:**
- Provide immediate, specific, constructive feedback
- Explain why consequences occurred
- Balance challenge with psychological safety
- Ensure logical connection between decisions and outcomes

### Inadequate Planning
**Symptoms:**
- Scope creep without resource adjustment
- Insufficient SME involvement
- Inadequate testing cycles
- Unrealistic timelines

**Solutions:**
- Develop realistic timelines accounting for iteration
- Define clear project scope with change management procedures
- Allocate sufficient time for testing and revision
- Educate stakeholders about development complexity

## Assessment Integration

### Embedded Assessment
- Assess through choices and consequences, not separate quizzes
- Track decision patterns and outcomes naturally
- Map performance to specific competencies

### Performance Analytics
- Pathway analysis: Common routes and outcomes
- Decision patterns: Information accessed, time spent
- Help-seeking behavior: When and how support is used
- Outcome achievement: Learning objectives met

### Feedback Systems
- Adaptive feedback based on performance patterns
- Additional support for consistent struggles
- Advanced challenges for high performers
- Targeted resources for identified gaps

## Quality Assurance Checklist

**Content Validation:**
- [ ] All factual information verified by SMEs
- [ ] Decisions reflect real-world practitioner choices
- [ ] Consequences logically connected to decisions
- [ ] Characters avoid stereotypes and show diversity

**Pedagogical Effectiveness:**
- [ ] Activities align with learning objectives
- [ ] Difficulty progresses appropriately
- [ ] Feedback is immediate, specific, and constructive
- [ ] Assessment embedded throughout experience

**Technical Functionality:**
- [ ] All pathways tested and functional
- [ ] Cross-platform compatibility verified
- [ ] Accessibility standards met
- [ ] Performance acceptable across devices

**User Experience:**
- [ ] Navigation intuitive and clear
- [ ] Instructions and feedback comprehensible
- [ ] Engagement maintained throughout
- [ ] Completion time reasonable

## Development Workflow

1. **Needs Analysis:** Identify performance gaps and authentic contexts
2. **Learning Objectives:** Define measurable, behavior-focused outcomes
3. **Decision Points:** Map critical real-world decision moments
4. **Storyboarding:** Visualize pathways and consequences
5. **SME Validation:** Verify authenticity and accuracy
6. **Alpha Testing:** Internal team review and bug fixing
7. **Beta Testing:** Target audience feedback and refinement
8. **Deployment:** Monitor analytics and gather feedback
9. **Iteration:** Continuous improvement based on data

## Advanced Considerations

**Adaptive Elements:**
- Adjust difficulty based on demonstrated competence
- Provide remediation for struggling learners
- Offer advanced challenges for high performers

**Collaborative Scenarios:**
- Multi-user experiences for teamwork training
- Role-based collaboration with interdependencies
- Peer review and feedback mechanisms

**Immersive Technologies:**
- VR/AR for spatial awareness and high-risk training
- Haptic feedback for procedural skills
- Multi-sensory integration for enhanced retention

## Key Principles Summary

1. **Meaningful Choices:** 3-5 plausible options requiring genuine analysis
2. **Realistic Consequences:** Logically connected, showing multiple effects
3. **Progressive Complexity:** Start simple, build gradually with fading support
4. **Authentic Context:** Reflect real-world complexity and stakeholders
5. **Embedded Reflection:** Throughout experience, not just at end
6. **Immediate Feedback:** Specific, constructive, explaining causation
7. **Avoid Over-Complexity:** Balance authenticity with cognitive capacity
8. **Iterative Development:** Test, gather feedback, refine continuously
